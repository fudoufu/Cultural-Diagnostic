## Email/Comms Material

**Welcome – and thank you for taking part.**

This Employee Voice Survey is part of the cultural improvement work across Somerset Council.

**This is the main way we're hearing from staff this year.** It builds on last year's staff survey but takes a different shape – it tells us how we really work across the council, what's helping and what isn't, so that we can make meaningful improvements.

There are no right or wrong answers here – every role and team is different, and your honest reflections matter in shaping the future of how we work.

The survey takes around 15 minutes to complete. The questions are split into three parts:

- What you see happening around you day-to-day
- What you tend to do in work situations yourself
- Your experience at work

We'll share back what we've found in May and invite you for more inputs. We look forward to seeing your responses – it'll help shape how we work together going forward.

Your answers will be anonymous. However, where there is the opportunity to add free text, please be aware that what you say here may make you identifiable.

Any questions? Contact [cindia.li@somerset.gov.uk](mailto:cindia.li@somerset.gov.uk).

## Survey Landing Page

Welcome and thank you for taking part in this year's **Our Culture – Employee Voice Survey 2026**.

This survey is the starting point for our Culture Discovery work. It will help shape the wider **Our Cultural Change** programme across Somerset Council.

This year, it replaces the usual staff survey and is the main way we're hearing from colleagues about what's working well and where we can make meaningful improvements.

The survey takes around 15 minutes to complete and has three parts. It asks about:

Thank you for taking the time to have your say – your voice matters.

Think about the part of the council that most shapes your day-to-day work – that might be your team, your service, or beyond. Answer based on what you see most in your daily experience.

**Response scale:** 1 = Strongly Agree | 2 = Agree | 3 = Neither agree nor disagree | 4 = Disagree | 5 = Strongly disagree

**Which part of the council most shapes your day-to-day work?**

- My directorate
- My service
- My immediate team
- Other _(open text)_

**Please rate the following statements:**

_Set 1_

- Teamwork comes first here – it's about what we achieve together, not individually.
- People are accountable for their own work and credited for their results.

_Set 2_

- Decisions are mostly made by senior people and passed down.
- People are trusted and encouraged to make decisions at their level.

_Set 3_

- People's wellbeing comes first in everyday work.
- People make decisions based on what will get the best results.

_Set 4_

- It's okay to question decisions and suggest different approaches.
- People tend to avoid conflict, even when something needs to be said.

_Set 5_

- There are processes we must follow in how we work.
- People are free to step outside the process based on what's needed.

_Set 6_

- People tend to keep doing things the way they've always been done.
- People are encouraged to try new things and new ways of working.

_Set 7_

- People prioritise what will deliver results soon rather than further down the line.
- People consider what something will mean months or years down the line, not just right now.

_Set 8_

- People are always looking for ways to do things better around here.
- We tend to fix things after they go wrong, not before.

_Set 9_

- People mostly talk to each other when there's a specific task to deal with.
- Building good relationships is seen as an important part of the job here.

_Set 10_

- What we do and when we do it is mostly planned out in advance.
- The work shapes the day – we change what we do based on what's needed at the time.

_Set 11_

- People get credit for what they actually do and contribute.
- How senior you are tends to affect how people work with you and treat you.

## Section 2 – How You Tend to Work

This section is about you – how you naturally tend to work, think and approach situations. Answer based on what feels true to you, not what your role asks of you. Everyone is different, and there are no right or wrong answers.

**Please rate the following statements:**

_Set 1_

- I'm more focused on what we achieve as a team than what I achieve personally.
- I like to be responsible for my own work and get on with it independently.

_Set 2_

- I prefer to know that the right people have approved a decision before moving forward.
- When something needs doing, I'm comfortable making a call and getting on with it.

_Set 3_

- When I have to make a tough decision, I think about the impact on my colleagues first.
- Getting the job done is my main priority.

_Set 4_

- When I disagree with something, I like to raise it.
- I prefer to keep the peace and avoid disagreements.

_Set 5_

- I work best when there's a clear process to follow.
- I'm comfortable changing how I work depending on what the situation needs.

_Set 6_

- I prefer to stick with ways of doing things that I know work.
- I often think about how things could be done differently.

_Set 7_

- I focus on what needs to get done today rather than further ahead.
- I think about where things are heading in the long run whenever I make decisions about today.

_Set 8_

- I seek out opportunities to learn and improve regularly.
- I mostly learn when something goes wrong and I have to figure it out.

_Set 9_

- Most of my conversations with colleagues are about work.
- I like to have close connections with my colleagues.

_Set 10_

- I like to know the plan upfront and work to it.
- I prefer to keep plans flexible and have my work shape my day.

_Set 11_

- I care more about what someone can contribute than how long they've been here or what their job title is.
- I'm conscious of people's seniority and title when working with them.
